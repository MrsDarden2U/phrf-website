About the program

Designed for teens who are ready to grow

The Potter's House Relief Fund Youth Program is a structured, supportive program serving inner-city and minority teens across Ohio, Kentucky, and Georgia. We partner with local churches, schools, and community organizations to reach young people where they are and give them a clear path forward.

Our program is built on the belief that every teen, regardless of their background or circumstance, deserves access to real opportunity. Through hands-on training, one-on-one mentorship, and academic support, we walk alongside young people as they discover their potential and take steps toward a meaningful future.

What we offer

Program offerings

Every participant has access to a full range of services at no cost to the family.

💼

Job Training and Workforce Readiness

Real-world workforce readiness including resume writing, interview skills, professional communication, and career exploration across multiple industries.

🤝

Mentoring

Consistent, trusted mentors walk alongside each teen, providing accountability, encouragement, and a positive role model throughout the program.

📚

Academic tutoring

Subject-specific tutoring support to help teens improve grades, build study habits, and stay on track for graduation and beyond.

🎓

College Grant Support

Guidance on college applications, financial aid, and grant opportunities to make higher education accessible for every participant.

Who qualifies

Eligibility requirements

Our program is open to teens who meet the following criteria. If you have questions about eligibility, please reach out. We are happy to help.

  • Between the ages of 13 and 17
  • Residing in Ohio, Kentucky, or Georgia
  • From an inner-city or minority community
  • Committed to attending scheduled sessions
  • Parent or guardian consent and involvement
  • No prior program experience required
