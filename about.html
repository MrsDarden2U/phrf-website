About PHRF

Our Story.
Rooted in purpose.

The Potter's House Relief Fund did not begin in a boardroom. It began in the life of a teenager who needed someone to believe in him, and did not find that person until much later than he should have.

The name behind the mission

The Potter's House

When founder Duane Darden set out to name this organization, he turned to scripture. In the Book of Jeremiah, the prophet is sent to a potter's house to receive a message from God. He watches as the potter works a piece of clay on the wheel. The clay becomes marred. It is damaged, misshapen, and seemingly ruined. But the potter does not throw it away. He simply begins again, patiently reshaping the clay into a new vessel, one with purpose and value.

"So I went down to the potter's house, and I saw him working at the wheel. But the pot he was shaping from the clay was marred in his hands; so the potter formed it into another pot, shaping it as seemed best to him."

Jeremiah 18:3-4

That image became the foundation for everything PHRF does. The teenagers we serve are not broken beyond repair. They are clay on the wheel. Some have been marred by poverty, trauma, absent parents, or systems that failed them before they were old enough to advocate for themselves. Our job is to be the potter's hands. Patient. Steady. Committed to the process of reshaping.

Where we came from

Born in Cincinnati. Built for the nation.

The Potter's House Relief Fund traces its roots to Cincinnati, Ohio, a city where the need for youth support and family stabilization has always been acute. The organization was founded by Duane Darden, a man who grew up in Cincinnati navigating many of the same circumstances the teens we serve face today.

Duane did not start this organization from the outside looking in. He built it from the inside out, drawing on his own lived experience to create something that addresses not just the surface needs of young people, but the deeper ones. The longing to be seen. The need for a stable adult presence. The hunger for a future that feels possible.

From that Cincinnati foundation, the National Youth Network grew to serve teens and families across three states, carrying the same mission into Kentucky and Georgia. Each region was chosen because the community need was present and the opportunity to make a real difference was clear.

Ohio

Where the story began

Cincinnati was the starting point for PHRF, and Ohio remains the heart of the network. We serve teens and families in Cincinnati, Dayton, and Columbus, partnering with local churches and schools in each city.

Kentucky

Crossing the river

Just across the Ohio River from Cincinnati, Covington was a natural next step. The network expanded through Kentucky to reach teens in Covington, Lexington, and Louisville, communities with strong ties to the Ohio service area.

Georgia

Growing the network south

Atlanta became PHRF's southeastern hub, with service extending to Macon and Savannah. Georgia's urban communities mirror the challenges and opportunities our network was built to address.

Our mission

"To provide emergency relief to families in crisis and equip teens ages 13 to 17 with the mentorship, job training, academic support, and life skills they need to build a future they are proud of."

The Potter's House Relief Fund, National Youth Network
