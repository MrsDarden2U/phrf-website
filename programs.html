What we do

Programs built to
lift and equip.

Four interconnected programs designed to meet teens and families where they are, and walk with them toward something better.

All programs serve teens ages 13 to 17 and their families across Ohio, Kentucky, and Georgia. Enrollment through the PHRF National Youth Network.

01

Program one

Emergency Financial Assistance

Disaster Relief  |  Eviction Prevention  |  Food Assistance

When a family hits a wall, the last thing they need is a waitlist. PHRF's Emergency Financial Assistance program provides direct financial aid to teens and their households facing immediate crisis, stabilizing the home environment so a young person can focus on growing, not surviving.

We provide disaster relief for families displaced or disrupted by unforeseen events, eviction prevention funds for households facing housing instability, and food assistance for teens experiencing food insecurity. Every dollar goes directly to the family, paired with referrals to additional community resources to address the root cause, not just the symptom.

  • Direct cash assistance, no intermediary markup
  • Covers disaster relief, rent/utility arrears, and food costs
  • Grocery gift cards or food pantry access for enrolled teens
  • Paired with referrals to local support organizations

Program two

Job Training and Workforce Readiness

Resume Writing  |  Interview Prep  |  Financial Literacy  |  Life Skills  |  Soft Skills

Most teens age out of systems without knowing how to write a resume, handle a job interview, or manage a paycheck. PHRF's Job Training and Workforce Readiness program closes that gap with a full-year curriculum built around the practical skills teens actually need to enter the workforce with confidence.

A single, consistent instructor delivers five sessions per month across a 12-month program year. That consistency is intentional. Relationship continuity with teens improves attendance, engagement, and outcomes. Quarterly guest speakers from the local business community, including HR professionals, hiring managers, and entrepreneurs, bring real-world perspective that no classroom can fully replicate.

Teens leave the program with a completed resume, hands-on mock interview experience, and a working understanding of financial basics. These are not soft skills. These are survival skills.

  • Resume writing and interview preparation
  • Financial literacy and money management
  • Life skills and professional soft skills
  • Mock interview sessions with real feedback

Program three

Mentoring Program

Community Volunteers  |  Small Group Model  |  Structured and Relational

Sometimes what a teenager needs most is not a program. It is a person. PHRF's Mentoring Program connects teens with trusted community adults, including business professionals, faith leaders, and committed community members, who show up consistently and care about what happens next.

Groups are small by design. One volunteer mentor leads a group of five teens, meeting monthly in person or online. Sessions blend structured activities with open relational discussion, giving teens space to be honest about what they are facing. Mentors are trained and supported by PHRF, and every teen in the program receives a goal tracking journal to document their growth across the year.

At the end of each program year, all enrolled teens participate in an end-of-summer group outing as a celebration of community, progress, and belonging. For many of these teens, it is the first time an adult has planned something special just for them.

  • Small groups of 5 teens per volunteer mentor
  • Monthly sessions, in-person or hybrid
  • Goal tracking journals for every enrolled teen
  • Annual end-of-summer celebration outing

Program four

Academic Tutoring and College Grant Support

1-on-1 Tutoring  |  FAFSA Navigation  |  Scholarship Support  |  Campus Visit

For a first-generation college-bound student, the distance between a C average and a scholarship application can feel like a canyon. PHRF's Academic Tutoring and College Grant Support program builds the bridge, providing weekly one-on-one tutoring and hands-on college access support for teens who would otherwise have to figure it out alone.

Tutoring is delivered online by college student contractors who bring recent academic experience and a relatable peer dynamic. Every teen in the tutoring program receives one hour of weekly support, plus additional on-call hours for test preparation and academic emergencies. All enrolled teens, including those not in the tutoring program, receive college planning workbooks, FAFSA completion support, and scholarship navigation guidance through PHRF's partner organizations.

Once a year, PHRF organizes a college campus visit day for tutoring participants. Research consistently shows that a single campus visit is one of the strongest predictors of college enrollment for first-generation students. We do not leave that to chance.

  • Weekly 1-on-1 tutoring delivered by college student contractors
  • On-call tutoring support for tests and academic emergencies
  • FAFSA completion workshops and scholarship navigation
  • Annual college campus visit day trip
